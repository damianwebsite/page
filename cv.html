About Me

I am a plant scientist, photographer, and creative communicator with a deep-rooted passion for biodiversity and the natural world. My work sits at the intersection of science and storytelling — I aim to document, understand, and communicate the beauty and importance of plant diversity.

Inspired by the legacy of David Attenborough and the fearless curiosity of Steve Irwin, I believe that science communication is not just about sharing facts — it's about igniting wonder, fostering connection, and inspiring action. Whether through research, photography, or interactive art, I strive to make the hidden world of plants visible, accessible, and captivating.

I am currently seeking a position where I can creatively stimulate biodiversity awareness — from documenting plant diversity in the field to developing engaging educational content, exhibitions, or media projects. My goal is to bridge the gap between scientific research and public understanding, making biodiversity conservation a shared mission.
